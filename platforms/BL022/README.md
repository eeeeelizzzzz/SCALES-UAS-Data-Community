## Overview
This is an OU/NSSL CopterSonde-SWX platform. This specific UAS was used as the reference UAS during the intercomparison. 
Tail Number: FA3EFFW4YX 
Site(s): Washington/Kessler Atmospheric and Ecological Field Station (KAEFS)
Active Dates: 8 Sept (WASH), 9 Sept (WASH), 10 Sept (WASH), 11 Sept (WASH), 12 Sept (WASH), 13 Sept (WASH) 
Platform Type: UAS Atmospheric Profiler, quad copter

Aircraft specifications are documented in specs.yaml. The original CopterSonde uncrewed aerial system (UAS) was designed to sample the thermodynamic and kinematic state of the lower Earth’s atmosphere, with a focus on vertical profiles in the planetary boundary layer. It provides the same information as a rawinsonde, but with much more control of its sampling location. Development began in 2016, for the NOAA-funded EPIC (Environmental Profiling and Initiation of Convection) field campaign, and development has largely continued through support from the National Science Foundation, University of Oklahoma, and NOAA. The initial design has undergone considerable modification, and the CopterSonde UAS is now capable of adaptative atmospheric sampling, real-time data processing and dissemination, longer flight times, and better data quality. SCALES included three ditinct CopterSonde versions: CopterSonde-3D-operated under NOAA flight authorizations, CopterSonde 3-commercial availble system from iMet; CopterSonde-SWX-latest model with higher wind tolerance operated under OU flight authorizations. This platform follows the WMO-CF-1.0 conventions for meteorological data.
